# 📊 Key Business Insights – Danny Diner Case Study

## 💰 Customer Spending Behavior
- Customer A appears to be the highest spender based on total purchase value
- Customer B shows consistent purchasing behavior across multiple days
- Customer C has comparatively lower engagement and overall spending

---

## 🍣 Product Performance
- Sushi is the most frequently purchased item across customers
- Sushi also generates the highest loyalty points due to its 2x multiplier
- Ramen and curry contribute steadily but are less impactful in terms of points

---

## 📅 Customer Visit Patterns
- Some customers visit frequently but spend less per visit
- Others visit less often but have higher total spending
- This indicates different customer segments such as frequent visitors vs high-value customers

---

## 👥 Loyalty Program Impact
- Customers tend to spend more after becoming members
- Membership appears to increase repeat purchases and engagement
- The first-week double points offer strongly influences customer behavior

---

## 🎯 Business Recommendations

### 1. Focus on High-Value Customers
- Provide targeted offers and rewards to customers like A to retain them

### 2. Promote Popular Items
- Use sushi in promotions or combo deals to maximize revenue and engagement

### 3. Strengthen Loyalty Program
- Extending first-week bonus offers could further improve customer retention

### 4. Re-engage Low Activity Customers
- Customers like C can be targeted with personalized offers or discounts

---

## 🚀 Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates how customer transactions and product-level data can be used to generate meaningful business insights. These insights can help improve marketing strategies, enhance customer retention, and drive overall business growth.
